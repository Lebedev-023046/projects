Regular Expressions

Used for

  • search for specific elements
  • character replacement
  • input validation
  • coordination

Metasymbols and character ranges

RegExp: /.[oe]t/g

Result: He got off the bus and set off to home.

Multipliers

Examples

  • * - the value occurs 0 or more times
  • + - the value occurs 1 or more times
  • ? - the value occurs 0 or 1 time
  • {5} - the value occurs 5 times
  • {3,7} - the value occurs between 3 and 7 times
  • {2,} - the value occurs at least 2 times

RegExp: /b.*e/g

Result: To shoot the breeze.

character screening

RegExp: this\.

Result: Give me a book like this.

A little more about metasymbols

Examples

  • \s - corresponds to anything that is considered a space
  • \S - is the opposite of \s
  • \d - corresponds to any number from 0 to 9
  • \D - is the opposite of \d
  • \w - corresponds to any string character: [A-Za-z0-9_]
  • \W - is the opposite of \w

RegExp: \d{2}

Result: I need to pay all my debts to Mishel($50) and Ragnar($90)

Anchors

Examples

  • ^ - represents the beginning of the string
  • $ - represents the end of the string

RegExp: ^\d+

Result: 6 women and 5 men were invited to a meeting.

Flags

Example: (/.+/g)

Examples

  • g - global search
  • i - ignore case
  • m - multiline
